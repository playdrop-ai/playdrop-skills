# Pitch And Validation

Use this when turning a vague idea into a real Playdrop concept before implementation starts.

## Pitch checklist

- one-sentence promise
- target player, best platform, and likely session shape
- proven core loop and why players will understand it quickly
- first-15-second beat that should create desire, not just comprehension
- why this is better than or meaningfully different from the strongest game in the same lane
- why the fantasy, art, and marketing promise intensify the same audience the loop serves

## Validation checklist

- inspect 2 to 3 Playdrop references before committing to the concept
- confirm the required surfaces exist on Playdrop: hosted runtime, auth, saves, realtime, monetization, AI assets
- identify the smallest playable slice that proves the idea
- identify the main production risk early: art, controls, performance, networking, or content volume
- kill or reshape the concept early if any of these are true:
  - it is still basically a famous game with a skin
  - the primary interaction grammar is novel enough to need teaching before the fun starts
  - the danger, reward, and fail state are easy to misread
  - the title, art, or pitch promise a fantasy the build will not honestly deliver
  - the likely player experience is a short toy with no real replay reason
  - the idea only becomes interesting after more content or marketing treatment

## Output

Produce:

- a short pitch paragraph
- a feasibility note
- a first-15-second beat
- a list of required platform features
- a recommendation to continue, reshape, or stop
